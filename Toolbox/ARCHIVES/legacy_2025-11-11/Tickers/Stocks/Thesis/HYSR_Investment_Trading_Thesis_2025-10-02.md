# SunHydrogen (HYSR) — Investment & Trading Thesis
**Date:** October 02, 2025

## What’s New (Near-Term Catalyst)
- **First field pilot:** HYSR is deploying a **>30 m²** outdoor **solar-to-hydrogen (PEC) array** at UT-Austin’s **Hydrogen ProtoHub**, with **GTI Energy** handling safety review and systems integration. The pilot runs **~6 months** to validate performance, durability, and scalability.
- **Module milestone:** HYSR previously demoed a **1.92 m² reactor** producing H₂ from **sunlight + water only** (off-grid). This is the building block for the 16‑reactor pilot.

## What HYSR Is (and Isn’t)
- **Photoelectrochemical (PEC) hydrogen:** sunlight drives water‑splitting directly in panel‑like reactors (nanoparticle/semiconductor absorbers + catalysts), **no separate electrolyzer + grid power** required.
- **Upside:** potential **fewer balance‑of‑plant components**, **distributed/off‑grid** footprint, simplified siting.
- **Risk:** **newer tech class** with **unproven commercial durability** vs. electrolyzers.

## Sector Setup (Tailwinds/Headwinds)
- **Policy tailwind:** U.S. IRA **45V** production tax credit (up to **$3/kg** for clean hydrogen) supports project bankability if lifecycle emissions are low enough.
- **Hydrogen hubs:** DOE‑backed regional hubs aim to accelerate deployment, though timelines and contracting can be uneven.
- **Electrolyzer costs:** Falling capex for conventional electrolyzers sets a **benchmark HYSR must beat** on cost per kg (LCOH) and reliability.

## Peer Context (How HYSR Differs)
- **Plug Power (PLUG):** Vertically integrated green H₂ + PEM electrolyzers; operating plants today; historically loss‑making but scaling.
- **Bloom Energy (BE):** Solid‑oxide fuel cells and electrolyzers; revenue‑generating; seen as AI/data‑center power adjacency.
- **Nel ASA (NEL):** Established alkaline/PEM electrolyzer OEM; cyclical orders/backlog dynamics.
- **SunHydrogen (HYSR):** **Pre‑revenue, PEC** tech path with a **pilot inflection**; value hinges on outdoor performance, durability, cost/kg, and scalable manufacturing.

## HYSR Fundamentals (Risk Profile)
- **Micro‑cap, OTCQB**, large share count; **pre‑revenue**; likely reliant on **equity financing** as it progresses from R&D → pilot → scale. High dilution risk typical for stage.

## Investment Thesis (12–36 Months)

### Bull Case
1. **Pilot validates** outdoor PEC performance (stable output, catalyst life, low fouling) → credible **LCOH pathway** versus solar+grid+electrolyzer stacks.
2. **GTI Energy/UT Austin** participation adds **safety/systems credibility**, easing **partner adoption** and access to grants/hubs.
3. **Policy tailwinds** (45V) + **distributed/off‑grid niches** (remote sites, industrial campuses, data‑center backup/peaker) create a lane where PEC can win on simplicity and siting.

### Bear Case
1. **Durability/maintenance:** fouling, photo‑absorber degradation, thermal cycling could **erode uptime** and increase $/kg.
2. **Scale economics:** Electrolyzers are **rapidly deflating in cost** and already bankable; PEC must clearly **beat BOS complexity** with convincing **$/kg & capex**.
3. **Financing/dilution:** Pre‑revenue micro‑caps often fund via equity; timeline slips increase **raise risk**.

### What Would Change the Thesis (Proof Points)
- **Measured pilot KPIs:** sustained H₂ rate per m², **capacity factor**, **module MTBF**, tolerance to real‑world water quality.
- **Third‑party techno‑economics:** audited **$/kg** versus co‑located solar + PEM electrolyzer.
- **Customer MoUs/LOIs:** campus/industrial/data‑center pilots **post‑ProtoHub**.
